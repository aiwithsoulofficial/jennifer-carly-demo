Quick Wins You Can Action Now

These are specific things we spotted during our call that you can start working on straight away - even before we officially kick off. Consider this your cheat sheet.

Ads + Landing Pages

One Ad = One Landing Page = One Booking Link

Your skin hydration ad currently drops people on the general treatments page with 10+ options. Your Morpheus ad goes to Timely with multiple Morpheus options and exosome add-ons. Every ad should go to its own dedicated landing page with one clear offer and one booking button. The person who clicked "fix my skin lines" should only see skin line results and one way to book.

Before and Afters Go at the Very Top

On your Evolve X postpartum landing page, the before-and-afters are buried below the fold. Flip it - results photos go in the hero position, right at the top. The "book now" button sits directly underneath. Everything else (how it works, FAQs, testimonials) goes below for people who need more nurturing before they commit. Your prime real estate is above the fold - use it for proof.

Make the Birthday Coupon Visible and Safe

Your 20% birthday offer looks great, but when someone clicks through to Timely to book, there's no sign of the 20% anywhere. They don't trust that the discount will be applied, so they abandon. Fix: give them a coupon code, and write "20% already applied at checkout" in multiple places on the landing page and in Timely. They need to feel safe that you know they came through the offer.

Simplify What People See in Timely

When someone clicks "book a consult" from any ad, they currently see every consultation type you offer - and they have no idea which one to pick. Especially new clients. They feel confused, then stupid, then they leave. Use Timely's individual service links so each ad sends people directly to the one specific booking option - no scrolling, no guessing, no decision fatigue.

High-Value Treatment Strategy

Gateway Treatment for Morpheus 8

Instead of promoting full-face Morpheus at a high price point, lead with one small treatment area people already know they have a problem with - like the double chin. Keep it under $500, clear time commitment, clear price. Once they're in the chair and love the result, that's when you introduce: "Did you know Morpheus can also help with tummy stretch marks? And combined with a little filler here and some anti-wrinkle there, we can get you that full glass-skin glow." Bums on seats first, upsell in person.

The "Glass Skin" Bundle

Position a signature package combining Morpheus (fixes texture on top) + filler (plumps and hydrates) + anti-wrinkle (smooths lines). Lead with the outcome language your clients are already searching for: "glass skin," "Korean skin," "dewy, glowy, hydrated." Anti-wrinkle gives immediate visible results while Morpheus works its magic over the following weeks - so clients see a transformation straight away and stay engaged for the full journey.

Evolve X Entry Point

The current campaign cost $4K to set up and got 2 bookings. Instead of promoting the full Evolve X experience at a premium price, offer a single-area trial at a gateway price point. One area, one session, clear result. Get them in, show them what the machine can do, then convert to a package. That machine needs to pay for itself - and this is how.

Social Media + Content

Fill Your Instagram Feed with Before and Afters

When someone sees your ad and clicks through to your Instagram (and they will - it's the first thing people do to check if a clinic is legit), they're looking for one thing: proof. Before-and-afters of real results. Your feed has beautiful aesthetic content, but it's not showing enough results. Whatever treatment you're currently advertising, flood your feed with B&As of that specific treatment. Clearly labelled at the top of each post: "Morpheus 8 results" or "IPL results." Skin and body before-and-afters are completely fine to post.

IPL Is Your Right-Now Seasonal Play

Right now is peak IPL season. Position it as: "Fix your pigmentation, redness, and those little veins around your nose NOW before summer - because IPL needs two weeks out of the sun, so winter is your window." Seasonal urgency + specific pain points + a clear reason to act now. This one should be front and centre on your socials and in a dedicated ad immediately.

Remarketing + Database

Separate Remarketing Campaign for Existing Clients

Your birthday 20% off is a brilliant offer - but it works best as a remarketing campaign targeting people who already know, like, and trust you. Export your Timely client list, upload it to Meta as a custom audience, and run the birthday offer specifically to past clients who haven't rebooked. New clients who've never heard of you won't respond to "20% off" the same way - it doesn't mean anything to them yet. Separate the campaigns: warm audience gets the loyalty offers, cold audience gets the gateway treatments.

Birthday SMS Cleanup Campaign

A lot of your Timely birthday data is the client's first appointment date, not their real birthday. Here's the campaign: send an SMS that says something like - "Have we ever missed your birthday? First of all, tell us when it is so we have it right - and secondly, we are SO sorry. We care about celebrating YOU and want to make it up to you with [offer]." It's an apology that builds rapport, gives them a reason to reply with their real birthday, AND a gift that brings them back through the door. You'll clean your database and reactivate lapsed clients in one hit.

Your Recall SMS Wording Is Already Gold

The opt-out line your FOH added to recall texts - asking clients to let you know if they want to be removed from the list - is genius. People responded with "no, keep me on, I'm just pregnant" or "I'm building a house." That tells you they want to come back. Now automate that: tag them as "returning later," set a 3-month or 6-month follow-up, and when the time's right, send a warm "ready to come back?" message. Don't lose them just because the timing wasn't right today.

Timely as Your Intelligence Engine

Turn Session Notes Into Revenue

Your team records session notes in Timely via voice-to-text - that's great data. But right now it sits there and nobody cross-references it. Imagine an AI agent that reads your session notes and checks them against what the client purchased, what treatment plans they've booked, and what they've paid for. "This client was recommended a course of 3 Morpheus sessions but only booked 1." "This client's skin plan includes Vitamin C serum but they haven't purchased it." "This client finished their treatment plan 6 weeks ago and hasn't rebooked." Every gap is a warm follow-up your front of house can action immediately - not a cold call, a real "we noticed you haven't finished your plan, how's your skin going?" conversation.

Treatment Plan Compliance Tracking

When you prescribe a multi-session plan (like 3x Morpheus or a course of peels), how do you know who's fallen off halfway through? Right now - you probably don't unless someone manually checks. An automated system can flag every incomplete treatment plan, every lapsed client who was mid-journey, and every product recommendation that hasn't been purchased. Your front of house gets a daily list: "These 5 clients need a follow-up today." No digging through records. No guessing. Just a clean, prioritised action list.

Client History at Your Fingertips

Before a client walks in, imagine your therapist gets a one-page brief: what they've had done, what was recommended, what products they're using, how long since their last visit, any notes from previous sessions. No scrolling through Timely. No "remind me what we did last time?" Just walk in informed, pick up where you left off, and make the client feel remembered. That's how you build the kind of loyalty that your 5-star reviews already show - at scale, across both locations, with every team member.

Customer Flow - From First Click to Loyal Client

Map the Full Journey (and Close the Gaps)

Right now the customer flow looks something like: see an ad > click through > land on website or Timely > maybe book, maybe not > if they book, they come in > treatment happens > session notes go into Timely > and then... it depends. Sometimes there's a follow-up. Sometimes there isn't. Sometimes they rebook on the spot. Sometimes they don't and nobody chases it. We map this entire journey end-to-end - every touchpoint from first click to repeat client - and identify every gap where someone can fall out. Then we plug those gaps with automation: instant follow-ups, treatment plan reminders, product recommendations, rebooking prompts, review requests, birthday campaigns. The whole thing runs like clockwork without adding to anyone's workload.

Max 2-3 Offers on the Homepage

Your homepage currently shows everything you do. That's great for you - but overwhelming for someone who's never been. Give cold visitors two or three clear paths: "I'm new and I want a consultation," "I know what I want - book a treatment," or "I'm not sure yet - take our Skin Assessment." Everyone else gets filtered through. Keep the full treatment menu for people who are already deep in your site. Your homepage is a doorway, not a catalogue.

"Just Book a General Consultation" Ad Angle

Record a video where you speak directly to the camera and say: "I know there are a hundred treatments out there and you have no idea which one is right for you. That's exactly why we offer a consultation. You don't need to know what you need - that's our job. Just come in, tell us what's bothering you, and we'll create your plan." That one video, with a direct link to a single "General Consultation" booking in Timely, will outperform almost any treatment-specific ad for cold audiences. People don't want to choose - they want to be guided.

Retention - The Client Never Leaves

Post-Treatment Check-Ins That Build Loyalty

48 hours after every treatment, an automated SMS goes out: "Hey [name], how's your skin feeling after your [treatment] on Tuesday? Any questions at all, just text us back." Simple, personal, and it does three things: shows you care, catches any concerns early, and keeps you top of mind. A week later: "Your skin should be starting to show results now - we'd LOVE to see how it's looking! Send us a photo?" That gives you fresh before-and-after content straight from happy clients AND makes them feel like part of something special.

Special Event Reminders + Photo Requests

If a client mentions they have a wedding, birthday, holiday, or event coming up, tag it in the system. Then an automated SMS goes out a few days before: "Your [event] is this weekend! We hope you're feeling absolutely amazing. Can't wait to see photos!" And a day after: "HOW WAS IT?! We want to see photos!" It's warm, it's fun, and it connects the treatment results to a real moment in their life. That emotional link is what turns a one-time client into a lifelong one. And every photo they send back? Content gold.

Compliance + Quick Wins

Your Before-and-Afters Are AHPRA-Safe

We know you pulled all before-and-afters off your socials because of AHPRA concerns. Here's the good news: skin and body treatment results are completely fine to post. The restriction is on injectables only. That means Morpheus 8, IPL, microneedling, chemical peels, dermaplaning, LED, Evolve X, CO2 - all of those results can go back on your feed immediately. And they should, because before-and-afters are the single most effective content type for aesthetic clinics. Get them back up as soon as possible.

Match Your Feed to Your Current Promo

Whatever treatment you're currently advertising, your Instagram feed should be flooded with before-and-afters of that specific treatment. Running a Morpheus ad? Your last 5-6 posts should include Morpheus results. Promoting IPL this month? IPL results everywhere. When someone sees your ad and clicks through to your profile (and they will - it's the first thing people do to vet a clinic), they should see proof of the exact thing they just clicked on. It builds instant trust and closes the loop between ad and action.

Export Timely Database for Meta Remarketing

Your entire client database in Timely is a goldmine for Meta advertising. Export it, upload it as a custom audience, and you can run targeted campaigns specifically to past clients (birthday offers, "we miss you" campaigns, new treatment launches). Even better - Meta can create a lookalike audience from your best clients, so your ads reach new people who look just like the ones who already love you. This is one of the highest-ROI moves in clinic marketing and it costs nothing extra in ad spend.

Staff + Consults

Staff-Specific Campaigns

Your girls each have a treatment they're passionate about - skin needling, Morpheus, CO2. Turn that into targeted marketing. Run ads featuring each therapist with their specialty: "[Name]'s Morpheus 8 results" with a booking link that goes directly to their calendar. When their books are quiet, turn the campaign on. When they're full, turn it off. Every girl builds her own client base, and you can balance capacity across the team without lifting a finger.

Payment Plan Positioning for Premium Packages

You mentioned clients weren't keen on payment plans before - but the way they're positioned makes all the difference. For premium packages ($3K+), use providers like Humm or Latitude where you get paid upfront in full, and the finance company carries the risk - not you. Position it as: "We've made it easy - pay over 12 months, interest-free, and your treatment starts this week." The key is making it feel like a convenience, not a financial hurdle. We've done this successfully with dental clinics for $10K+ veneers - it works when the script is right.
